How the Trust Score Updates

The Trust Score is designed to reward improving behavior, not just punish past failures.

Score updates immediately when:

  • A vendor participates in an interview after initially declining
  • A certification is independently verified
  • A vendor proactively discloses an incident
  • A vendor submits a formal response to their review
  • Pricing transparency improves
  • Data rights policies are clarified

Every Trust Score change is logged with a date and reason, visible in the Trust Score History section of each tool's review page.

Understanding Score Gaps

When a significant gap (15+ points) exists between Product Score and Trust Score, we display a callout to help buyers interpret what it means.

When Product Score exceeds Trust Score by 15+ points: "High performer, low transparency ‚Äî this tool scores well on capability but the vendor has limited verifiable transparency. Enterprise buyers in regulated industries should review the Trust Score breakdown before procurement."

When Trust Score exceeds Product Score by 15+ points: "High transparency, moderate performance ‚Äî this vendor demonstrates strong transparency practices. The product may have limitations but the vendor's integrity signals are positive."
