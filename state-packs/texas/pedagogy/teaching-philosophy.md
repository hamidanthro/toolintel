# Texas Pedagogical Philosophy — How Texas Teaches Each Subject

**Provenance:** [CLAUDE-SYNTHESIZED] from publicly-known TEA curriculum framework documents, the structure of the TEKS standards themselves (which encode pedagogical priorities), and observation of STAAR cognitive-demand distribution in released items.

This is a synthesis aid for content generation — when generating a question for grade X subject Y, the philosophy below tells the model what kind of thinking the question should exercise.

---

## Math

### Cognitive demand mix per grade

| Grade | Recall | Application | Analysis |
|---|---|---|---|
| 3 | 35% | 50% | 15% |
| 4 | 30% | 55% | 15% |
| 5 | 25% | 55% | 20% |
| 6 | 20% | 55% | 25% |
| 7 | 15% | 55% | 30% |
| 8 | 15% | 50% | 35% |
| Algebra I | 10% | 45% | 45% |

### Conceptual vs procedural balance

Texas TEKS prioritize **CONCEPTUAL understanding before procedural fluency**. The standards explicitly use phrases like "compose and decompose," "represent with concrete and pictorial models," "explain the relationships," and "describe the process." Procedural fluency (algorithmic mastery) is built ON TOP of conceptual foundation.

**Generation guidance:**
- Lower grades (3-5): more concrete-model questions (pictorial, number-line, partitioning). Fewer "compute X+Y" bare-fact questions.
- Middle grades (6-8): conceptual transfer to algebraic/symbolic representation. Rate of multi-step problems increases.
- Algebra I: balanced — solve equations, but also analyze structure, justify steps, identify relationships.

### Texas-specific math priorities

- **Personal financial literacy** is REQUIRED at every grade 3-8 (TEKS x.9 / x.10 / x.13 / x.14). Texas added this in 2012 and it's a distinguishing feature. Topics: income, taxes, banking, credit, simple/compound interest, budgeting, profit. **Generation guidance: each grade should have ~10% financial-literacy content from the financial-literacy strand.**
- **Process standards** (TEKS x.1) appear at every grade — apply mathematics to problems in everyday life, communicate mathematical ideas, use multiple representations, justify reasoning. These are "habits of mind" that ALL question types should exercise.
- **Strip diagrams** are a Texas signature — bar models for representing addition/subtraction/multiplication relationships. Use them at grades 3-5 routinely.

### What Texas math AVOIDS at each grade

- Grade K-2: no negative numbers, no decimals beyond money
- Grade 3-5: no negative numbers, no algebra-style variables (use "?" or boxes for unknowns)
- Grade 6-7: introduces integers and rational numbers but no irrationals
- Grade 8: introduces irrationals (√2, π in concept) but no logarithms, no trigonometric functions
- Algebra I: no calculus, no advanced functions (logs/trig come in Algebra II)

## English Language Arts and Reading (ELAR)

### Genre balance per grade

Texas TEKS specify a literary-vs-informational ratio that shifts with grade:

| Grade | Literary | Informational |
|---|---|---|
| 3 | 60% | 40% |
| 4 | 55% | 45% |
| 5 | 50% | 50% |
| 6 | 45% | 55% |
| 7 | 40% | 60% |
| 8 | 35% | 65% |
| English I/II | 30% | 70% (with arg/persuasive emphasis) |

### Cognitive demand by grade

ELAR cognitive demand is dominated by INFERENCE and SYNTHESIS at all grades. Even grade 3 STAAR Reading expects "make inferences with evidence" as a high-frequency item type. The progression:

- Grade 3-4: single-passage inference, central idea, character traits, basic figurative language (similes only)
- Grade 5-6: cross-passage inference, theme (introduced), more figurative language (similes + metaphors), structural analysis introduced
- Grade 7-8: argumentative-text analysis (claim + evidence + reasoning), counterclaim recognition, sophisticated figurative language (extended metaphor, irony)
- English I/II: full rhetorical analysis, multi-source synthesis, evaluation of author's choices

### Texas-specific ELAR priorities

- **Multiple genres** explicitly required: fiction, drama, poetry, informational, argumentative, multimodal. STAAR tests rotate through these.
- **Author's purpose and craft** is its own strand (5) — Texas explicitly assesses why authors made specific choices, not just what the text says.
- **Foundational language** (strand 1) for K-5 includes phonics, fluency, vocabulary acquisition. Strand 1 doesn't directly appear on grade 3-5 STAAR but is foundational to comprehension.

### What Texas ELAR AVOIDS

- Religious texts as literary subjects (per `contexts-avoided.md`)
- Politically-charged contemporary topics (no current-events partisan analysis)
- Highly-specific contemporary cultural references that age out (no specific current movies/games/celebrities)
- Texts that require specialized prior knowledge to comprehend

## Science (grade 5, grade 8, Biology EOC)

### Cognitive demand mix

| Grade | Recall | Application | Analysis |
|---|---|---|---|
| 5 | 30% | 50% | 20% |
| 8 | 20% | 50% | 30% |
| Biology EOC | 15% | 40% | 45% |

### Hands-on vs textbook balance

Texas TEKS explicitly require **40% of instructional time** be spent on hands-on lab/field investigations at grades K-5, and **40%** at grades 6-8 (per TEC §28.002). This pedagogical commitment is reflected in STAAR with frequent **experimental design** questions — "what variable should be controlled?" "what's the dependent variable?" — that go beyond content recall.

**Generation guidance:**
- Grade 5: include scenario-based questions where a kid (or scientist) is observing, measuring, or experimenting with something
- Grade 8: include experimental-design questions where the student must identify variables, controls, valid procedure
- Biology EOC: mix content-recall with data analysis (graph reading, controlled experiments)

### Texas-specific science priorities

- **Conservation of mass / energy** runs through all grades' physical science standards
- **Earth-Sun-Moon system** is a recurring theme (grade 5, grade 8)
- **Texas-relevant ecology** examples are common in passages (Texas wildlife, Gulf ecosystems, Hill Country flora)
- **Engineering design process** is woven in (especially post-2017 TEKS revision)

### What Texas science AVOIDS

- Climate change as a politicized topic (factual data is OK; cause-attribution debates are AVOIDED)
- Evolution as religiously-contested (TEKS Biology B.7A explicitly covers common ancestry; teach factually, not as debate)
- Reproductive biology beyond what TEKS B.10A explicitly covers
- Lab safety incidents, chemical accidents (use safe scenarios)

## Social Studies (grade 8 US History to 1877, US History EOC)

### Cognitive demand mix

| Test | Recall | Application | Analysis |
|---|---|---|---|
| Grade 8 US History | 25% | 50% | 25% |
| US History EOC | 15% | 50% | 35% |

### Skills strand emphasis

Texas social studies TEKS include a Skills strand (8.29-8.30 for grade 8; USH.29 for EOC) covering:
- Source evaluation (primary vs secondary)
- Map reading
- Chronological reasoning
- Cause-effect analysis
- Comparison and contrast

STAAR Social Studies includes high-frequency map-reading and primary-source-excerpt items. **Generation guidance:** ~25% of generated SS questions should include a stimulus (map, primary source, chart).

### Texas-specific social studies priorities

- **Founding documents** (Constitution, Declaration, Bill of Rights, Federalist Papers) are heavily emphasized
- **Free enterprise / market economy** is taught explicitly (TEKS USH.14A) as a positive feature of US economic system — Texas frames this directly
- **Civic virtue and citizenship** appear as their own strand
- **Geography integration** — students should connect physical geography to historical events (settlement patterns, economic development)

### What Texas SS AVOIDS

- See `contexts-avoided.md` for the complete list. Key:
- Modern political party characterizations
- Living politicians' policy positions
- Contested historiographical debates beyond what TEKS specifies
- Religion as a topic (the Pilgrims came for religious freedom IS factual; "America is a Christian nation" framing is NOT acceptable)
- Foreign policy debates with current relevance

## Cross-cutting pedagogical themes (all subjects)

### Process standards (math) / habits of mind (all subjects)

Texas TEKS embed "process standards" at every grade in math and similar habit-of-mind standards in other subjects:
1. Apply to problems arising in everyday life
2. Communicate ideas using multiple representations
3. Select tools, including technology
4. Analyze given information and create plans
5. Justify reasoning
6. Use mathematical/scientific/historical language

Generators should produce questions that exercise these habits, not just rote recall.

### Multiple representations

A consistent Texas pedagogy: students must work with the same idea in multiple representations:
- Math: verbal, numeric, tabular, graphical, algebraic
- Science: text, diagram, data table, model, equation
- ELAR: literal text, inferred meaning, evidence quote, paraphrase
- SS: timeline, map, primary source, secondary analysis

Mix representations across a generated bucket of questions.

### Real-world application

Texas explicitly favors real-world contexts over pure abstraction. STAAR items rarely use bare "find x" computation; they wrap math in scenarios. This is consistent across grades and subjects.

## What this means for the generator

For each generated question:
1. Choose the cognitive demand (low/medium/high) per the grade-subject mix above
2. For lower grades: prefer concrete/pictorial framing
3. For all grades: wrap in real-world scenario when possible
4. For Texas-flavored content: pull from `contexts-allowed.md`; avoid `contexts-avoided.md`
5. Math grades 3-8: include some financial-literacy questions (~10% of math output per grade)
6. ELAR: match the literary-vs-informational ratio per grade
7. Science grades 5/8: include experimental-design framing some of the time
8. SS grade 8 / USH EOC: include source/map/chart stimuli ~25% of the time
