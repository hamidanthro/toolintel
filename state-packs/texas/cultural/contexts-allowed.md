# Texas Cultural Contexts — ALLOWED

What STAAR draws from and what our generator should use to give Texas content authentic state flavor without violating STAAR neutrality.

**Provenance:** Synthesized from publicly-available knowledge of Texas geography/history/culture (Texas State Historical Association, Texas Almanac, US Census), TEA's STAAR-released test patterns, and the standing STAAR neutrality principle (politics off, religion off, contested history off — see `contexts-avoided.md`). Marked `[CLAUDE-SYNTHESIZED]` where applicable.

**Generation rule:** Texas-flavor content is OPTIONAL on STAAR-style questions. Most questions can be state-agnostic. When a question DOES use Texas flavor, it should pull from the items below — they are widely-recognizable, neutral, and STAAR-appropriate.

## 1. Geography

### Major regions
- **Gulf Coast** — Houston, Galveston, Corpus Christi, Brownsville. Shipping, fishing, hurricanes.
- **East Texas / Piney Woods** — Lufkin, Tyler, Nacogdoches. Pine forests, lumber industry.
- **Hill Country** — Austin (eastern edge), Fredericksburg, Kerrville. Bluebonnets, German heritage towns, Enchanted Rock.
- **Panhandle** — Amarillo, Lubbock. Plains, cotton, cattle, wind farms.
- **South Texas / Rio Grande Valley** — McAllen, Brownsville, Laredo. Citrus, bilingual culture, border heritage.
- **Big Bend / Trans-Pecos** — El Paso, Marfa. Desert, mountains, dark skies.
- **DFW Metroplex** — Dallas, Fort Worth, Arlington. Major commerce, sports, transportation hub.
- **Central Texas** — Waco, Temple, Killeen. Agricultural belt.

### Major rivers
Rio Grande, Brazos, Trinity, Colorado (Texas), Pecos, Red River, Sabine, San Antonio, Guadalupe, Nueces.

### Major cities (population order, broad familiarity)
Houston, San Antonio, Dallas, Austin (state capital), Fort Worth, El Paso, Arlington, Corpus Christi, Plano, Lubbock, Laredo, Garland, Irving, Amarillo, Grand Prairie, McKinney, Frisco, Brownsville, Pasadena, Killeen.

### Geographic features (STAAR-appropriate references)
- Big Bend National Park
- Padre Island National Seashore  
- Enchanted Rock
- Palo Duro Canyon
- Caddo Lake (East Texas)
- The Edwards Plateau
- The Ogallala Aquifer (geography/science)
- Galveston Bay
- The Trinity River system

## 2. History (uncontested, STAAR-safe)

### Pre-1500 / Native peoples
- Caddo, Comanche, Apache, Tonkawa, Karankawa, Coahuiltecan, Wichita peoples
- Mound-building cultures of East Texas
- Rock art at Lower Pecos (Caddoan)

### Spanish era (1500s–1821)
- Cabeza de Vaca's journey (~1528)
- Spanish missions (San Antonio missions: Mission San José, Mission Concepción, Mission San Juan, Mission Espada — these are now a UNESCO World Heritage site)
- The Camino Real
- The Alamo (Mission San Antonio de Valero, founded 1718) — **as a SETTING / HISTORIC SITE / ARCHITECTURE** is fine; the 1836 battle as a topic of Texas-state-pride or contested politics is NOT (see contexts-avoided.md)

### Mexican era (1821–1836)
- Mexican independence from Spain
- Texas as part of Mexico
- Stephen F. Austin and the "Old Three Hundred"

### Republic of Texas (1836–1845)
- Sam Houston
- The annexation by the United States in 1845

### Statehood through Civil War
- Pre-Civil War economy (cotton, cattle)
- The Civil War in Texas (factual events; see contexts-avoided.md for what NOT to litigate)
- Reconstruction

### Late 1800s–1900s
- Cattle drives and the Chisholm Trail
- King Ranch
- Spindletop oil discovery (1901) — Beaumont
- Galveston Hurricane of 1900
- Texas Rangers (law enforcement, factual)
- Discovery of the East Texas oil field (1930s)

### 20th century
- NASA Johnson Space Center (Houston) — space program
- Lyndon B. Johnson (born/raised in Texas Hill Country) — his Texas roots, NOT his political record (see contexts-avoided.md)
- Texas in WWII (military training, manufacturing)
- The Texas Medical Center (Houston) — largest medical complex in the world
- Population growth and urbanization

## 3. Industries (real-world contexts STAAR uses)

- **Agriculture**: cotton, cattle, beef, citrus (Rio Grande Valley), peaches, rice, wheat, sorghum, dairy
- **Oil and gas**: Permian Basin, Eagle Ford Shale, Houston refineries
- **Aerospace**: NASA JSC (Houston), Lockheed Martin (Fort Worth), Bell Helicopter (Fort Worth)
- **Technology**: Austin (Apple, Tesla, Dell, Samsung, IBM), Dallas-Fort Worth tech corridor
- **Shipping and ports**: Port of Houston (largest in the US by tonnage), Port of Corpus Christi
- **Healthcare**: Texas Medical Center, MD Anderson, Methodist
- **Higher education**: UT Austin, Texas A&M, Rice, Baylor, Texas Tech, UH
- **Music and entertainment**: Austin (Austin City Limits, SXSW), country and Tejano music heritage
- **Film**: Texas Film Commission, Austin film scene
- **Tourism**: Big Bend, Padre Island, San Antonio River Walk

## 4. Wildlife (STAAR-friendly nature contexts)

- **State symbols**: bluebonnet (state flower), mockingbird (state bird), longhorn (state large mammal), armadillo (state small mammal), Texas horned lizard (state reptile), monarch butterfly (state insect), pecan (state tree), prickly pear (state plant)
- **Common Texas animals**: white-tailed deer, coyote, jackrabbit, roadrunner, scissor-tailed flycatcher, great horned owl, painted bunting, javelina, alligator (East Texas), turkey vulture, snapping turtle
- **Marine**: Kemp's ridley sea turtle, brown pelican, dolphins (Gulf), redfish, speckled trout
- **Plants**: live oak, mesquite, prickly pear cactus, yucca, Spanish moss (East Texas), cedar (Hill Country), pecan
- **Bluebonnet season**: April; iconic Texas backdrop

## 5. Daily life and culture (STAAR-allowed contexts)

- **Food** (avoiding brand names): BBQ (brisket, ribs), Tex-Mex (enchiladas, fajitas, breakfast tacos), chili, kolache (East Texas Czech heritage), pecan pie, sweet tea
- **High school football** — culturally significant; Friday night games, marching bands
- **Rodeos and county fairs** — Houston Livestock Show and Rodeo, State Fair of Texas (Dallas), San Antonio Stock Show
- **Ranching and ranch life** — fence-mending, cattle drives, branding
- **Boots, hats, denim** — clothing references in scenarios
- **Bluebonnet field photos** in spring (very common Texas reference)
- **Lone Star** — the state symbol; appears on flag, on most state-government materials
- **"Don't Mess With Texas"** — the state's anti-littering campaign (originally), now also a cultural phrase. Use cautiously; can be read as state pride.

## 6. Authentic Texas place names

Mix of Spanish, English, and Native origin reflects Texas's heritage:

- **Spanish-origin**: San Antonio, El Paso, Amarillo (yellow), Laredo, Corpus Christi (body of Christ — neutral despite religious origin; widely used place name), Brazos (arms), Galveston (named for Spanish Bernardo de Gálvez), Pasadena
- **English-origin**: Houston (after Sam Houston), Dallas, Austin (after Stephen F. Austin), Fort Worth, Lubbock, Tyler, Plano (Spanish "flat" but used as English place name), Frisco
- **Native-origin**: Waxahachie, Nacogdoches, Lipantitlan, Comanche (county), Wichita (Falls)
- **German heritage** (Hill Country): Fredericksburg, New Braunfels, Comfort, Boerne (the latter two via German settlers; usually pronounced "BURN-ee"), Schulenburg
- **Czech heritage** (Central Texas): West, Praha, Dubina

## 9. Generation guidance

When writing Texas-flavor content:

1. Use real Texas place names if needed for setting; otherwise stay generic
2. Use diverse names (per `authentic-names.json`) reflecting Texas demographics
3. Use STAAR-allowed scenarios (Section 8); avoid all items in `contexts-avoided.md`
4. Don't over-flavor — most STAAR questions are state-neutral. A grade-3 question can absolutely be "Sofia has 24 stickers..." without ANY Texas reference. Texas flavor is for variety, not requirement.
5. When using a Texas landmark or fact, ensure it's neutral (geography, architecture, wildlife, founding date) and not contested (causes of conflict, modern political significance)
