Contributor Code of Conduct

Five requirements that every ToolIntel expert contributor must uphold

1

Full Real Identity Disclosure

Contributors must use their full real name, professional title, and current employer or affiliation. Anonymous or pseudonymous contributions are not permitted.

  • Your name, title, and employer will be publicly displayed on all contributions
  • A verifiable LinkedIn or institutional profile is required
  • Misrepresentation of credentials is grounds for immediate removal
2

No Financial Relationship with Reviewed Tools

Contributors may not have any financial relationship with AI tool companies whose products they review. This prohibition is absolute and permanent for tools under review.

  • No employment, consulting, or advisory relationships
  • No equity, stock options, or profit-sharing arrangements
  • No speaking fees, travel reimbursement, or gifts exceeding $100
  • Previous relationships must be disclosed even if concluded
3

Direct Hands-On Experience Required

All findings must be based on your own direct testing and experience with the tools. Secondhand reports, rumors, or speculation are not acceptable.

  • Document your testing methodology clearly
  • Specify what you tested and for how long
  • Distinguish between features you tested personally vs. reviewed documentation
  • Do not extrapolate beyond your direct experience
4

Evidence-Based Disagreements

When disagreeing with ToolIntel's editorial scores, you must provide specific, verifiable evidence. Personal opinion without supporting data is not sufficient.

  • Cite specific features, behaviors, or documentation
  • Provide reproducible test cases where possible
  • Reference industry standards or regulatory requirements
  • "I feel" or "I believe" statements require supporting evidence
5

7-Day Conflict Disclosure

Any change in your professional circumstances that could create a potential conflict of interest must be disclosed within 7 days.

  • New employment or consulting relationships
  • Investment in AI tool companies
  • Advisory board appointments
  • Speaking engagements with compensation
  • When in doubt, disclose

Why These Standards Matter

ToolIntel exists because the AI tool market lacks independent, verified analysis. Expert contributions add domain-specific depth that general reviewers cannot provide. But this value depends entirely on trust. A single compromised contributor undermines the credibility of the entire network. These requirements protect that trust.

⚠️ Enforcement

Violations of this code result in immediate removal from the Expert Network. Serious violations (undisclosed conflicts, fabricated credentials, or evidence manipulation) will be publicly noted in our quarterly Transparency Report with the contributor's name and nature of violation.

Contributor Compensation

Expert contributors are unpaid volunteers who contribute to advance the quality of independent AI tool intelligence. ToolIntel does not compensate contributors financially. Contributors may not solicit business from vendors whose tools they have reviewed for a period of 12 months following publication.
