## Sea Turtles of the Texas Coast

Sea turtles have lived in the ocean for millions of years. These amazing reptiles swim through warm waters all around the world. Five different kinds of sea turtles visit the coast of Texas. The most common visitor is the Kemp's ridley sea turtle.

## Where Sea Turtles Live

Sea turtles spend most of their lives in the ocean. They swim through shallow bays and deep water. Female sea turtles come onto beaches to lay their eggs. Padre Island National Seashore in Texas is an important nesting place. Every year, Kemp's ridley turtles return to the same beaches where they were born.

Sea turtles can hold their breath for a long time. They dive deep to find food. When they need air, they swim to the surface. A sea turtle might stay underwater for five hours while resting.

## What Sea Turtles Eat

Different sea turtles eat different foods. Kemp's ridley turtles love to eat crabs. They use their strong jaws to crack open the shells. Green sea turtles eat seagrass and algae. Loggerhead turtles eat jellyfish, clams, and crabs.

Sea turtles do not have teeth. Instead, they have hard beaks. These beaks are perfect for grabbing and crushing their food. A sea turtle's diet changes as it grows. Baby sea turtles eat tiny floating animals. Adult sea turtles eat larger prey.

## Life on the Beach

Female sea turtles crawl onto sandy beaches at night. They dig deep holes with their back flippers. Each turtle lays about 100 eggs in the nest. Then she covers the eggs with sand and returns to the ocean.

The warm sand keeps the eggs safe. After about two months, baby sea turtles hatch. They are only two inches long. The babies dig their way out of the sand. They race toward the ocean as fast as they can.

Many dangers face baby sea turtles. Birds and crabs try to catch them on the beach. Fish wait in the shallow water. Only a few baby turtles survive to become adults.

## Growing Up

Sea turtles grow slowly. A Kemp's ridley turtle takes 10 to 15 years to become an adult. During this time, young turtles travel far across the ocean. They follow ocean currents to find food.

Adult sea turtles can be quite large. A loggerhead turtle can weigh 250 pounds. Green sea turtles can grow even bigger. The largest green sea turtle ever found weighed over 800 pounds.

## Helping Sea Turtles

Sea turtles face many challenges today. Fishing nets can trap them underwater. Plastic trash in the ocean can make them sick. Bright lights on beaches confuse baby turtles.

People in Texas work hard to protect sea turtles. Scientists at Padre Island help baby turtles reach the water safely. They also rescue sick or injured turtles. Beach cleanups remove harmful trash. When people turn off bright lights during nesting season, more baby turtles find their way to the ocean.

Sea turtles are an important part of ocean life. By protecting them, we keep our oceans healthy for everyone.

## Where the River Bends

Big Bend National Park sits in the far southwest corner of Texas. The Rio Grande flows along its southern edge. The river makes a big curve here. That curve gives the park its name.

The park is huge. It covers more than 800,000 acres. That makes it one of the largest national parks in the United States. Inside the park, you can find mountains, deserts, and canyons.

## Mountains and Deserts

The Chisos Mountains rise up in the center of Big Bend. These mountains are very old. Their peaks reach over 7,800 feet high. When you stand at the top, you can see for miles.

Around the mountains, the Chihuahuan Desert spreads out. This desert gets very little rain each year. The ground is rocky and dry. But many plants and animals live here.

## Desert Plants

More than 1,200 kinds of plants grow in Big Bend. Cacti are common. The prickly pear cactus has flat, round pads covered with sharp spines. The ocotillo looks like a bundle of thorny sticks. After rain, it grows green leaves and bright red flowers.

The lechuguilla plant is special to this desert. It has stiff, pointed leaves that form a rosette shape. This plant can live for many years without much water.

## Animals of Big Bend

Many animals make their homes in the park. Roadrunners race across the desert floor. These birds can run up to 20 miles per hour. They eat lizards, insects, and small snakes.

Javelinas roam in small groups. These pig-like animals have thick, bristly hair. They use their strong snouts to dig for roots and bulbs.

At night, bats fly out of caves in the canyon walls. Big Bend is home to at least 20 species of bats. They eat insects and help control the bug population.

## Birds in the Sky

Big Bend is an excellent place for bird watching. More than 450 bird species have been seen here. That is more than any other national park.

The peregrine falcon nests on cliff ledges. This bird is one of the fastest animals on Earth. It can dive at speeds over 200 miles per hour when hunting.

The Colima warbler only nests in a few places in the United States. Big Bend is one of them. Bird watchers travel from far away hoping to see this small, gray songbird.

## The Rio Grande

The Rio Grande forms the park's southern boundary. The river carved deep canyons through the rock over millions of years. Santa Elena Canyon has walls that rise 1,500 feet straight up.

The river provides water for many animals. Beavers build dams in quieter sections. Fish swim in the deeper pools. Plants grow thick along the banks.

## Visiting Big Bend

People come to Big Bend to hike, camp, and explore. The park is far from big cities. The nearest town is more than 100 miles away. That distance helps keep the park wild and quiet.

At night, the sky fills with stars. Big Bend has some of the darkest skies in the country. You can see the Milky Way stretch across the heavens.

Big Bend is a place where the desert meets the mountains and the river. It is a place where many different plants and animals find a way to survive.
