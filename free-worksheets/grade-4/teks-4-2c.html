Esteban is helping his uncle on their ranch in Texas. They have 245 cattle and plan to add another 156 cattle to their herd. How many cattle will they have in total after the new additions?

  1. 401 ✓
  2. 391
  3. 405
  4. 395

Why: To find the total number of cattle, add the number of existing cattle, 245, to the number of new cattle, 156. So, 245 + 156 = 401. Therefore, the correct answer is 401.

  1. 375 ✓
  2. 250
  3. 200
  4. 125

Why: To find the largest amount, compare the numbers: 250, 375, 125, and 200. The number 375 is greater than all the others, making it the largest amount Layla has collected.

Stella is collecting stamps of animals native to Texas. She has 1,245 stamps featuring white-tailed deer, 872 featuring roadrunners, and 1,563 featuring javelinas. What is the correct order of the total number of stamps Stella has from least to greatest?

  1. 872, 1,245, 1,563 ✓
  2. 1,245, 872, 1,563
  3. 1,563, 1,245, 872
  4. 872, 1,563, 1,245

Why: To compare the numbers of stamps, we see that Stella has 872 roadrunner stamps, 1,245 white-tailed deer stamps, and 1,563 javelina stamps. The least number is 872, followed by 1,245, and the greatest is 1,563. Therefore, the correct order from least to greatest is 872, 1,245, 1,563.

Emilio loves collecting different types of Texas plants. He has 248 live oaks, 156 mesquite trees, and 312 prickly pear cacti. What is the total number of plants that Emilio has in his collection?

  1. 716 ✓
  2. 600
  3. 300
  4. 400

Why: To find the total number of plants in Emilio's collection, we need to add the number of each type of plant together. We have 248 live oaks, 156 mesquite trees, and 312 prickly pear cacti. Adding these together: 248 + 156 + 312 = 716. Therefore, the correct answer is 716.

  1. 1,245 + 1,387 ✓
  2. 1,245 - 1,387
  3. 1,387 - 1,245
  4. 1,245 * 1,387

Why: To find the total number of seashells that Victoria and Ricardo have together, you need to add the number of seashells each of them has. The correct expression is 1,245 + 1,387, which gives the total of 2,632 seashells.

Daniel is raising funds for his school by selling lemonade. He sold 145 cups on Monday, 238 cups on Tuesday, and 187 cups on Wednesday. How many cups of lemonade did Daniel sell in total over these three days?

  1. 570 ✓
  2. 580
  3. 600
  4. 630

Why: To find the total number of cups sold, add the cups sold each day: 145 + 238 + 187. First, add 145 and 238 to get 383. Then, add 187 to 383, which equals 570. Therefore, Daniel sold a total of 570 cups of lemonade.
