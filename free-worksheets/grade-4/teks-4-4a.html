Sofia is making a fruit salad for her family reunion. She has 3 apples, 4 bananas, and 5 oranges. How many pieces of fruit does she have in total?

  1. 12 ✓
  2. 10
  3. 8
  4. 9

Why: To find the total number of pieces of fruit Sofia has, add the number of apples, bananas, and oranges together: 3 + 4 + 5 = 12. Therefore, the correct answer is 12.

  1. $40.25 ✓
  2. $39.25
  3. $41.25
  4. $38.25

Why: To find out how much money Amelia spent in total, you need to add the cost of her lunch to the cost of the souvenir. So, $25.75 plus $14.50 is equal to $40.25. Therefore, the correct answer is $40.25.

Zara is organizing a book fair at her school. She has a total of 30 books to display. If she has 14 fiction books, how many non-fiction books does she have?

  1. 16 ✓
  2. 14
  3. 30
  4. 12

Why: To find the number of non-fiction books, subtract the number of fiction books from the total number of books. Zara has 30 books total and 14 of them are fiction. So, 30 - 14 equals 16. Therefore, Zara has 16 non-fiction books.

Eduardo is selling pecans at a local farmer's market in Texas. He sold 125 pounds of pecans in the morning and 238 pounds in the afternoon. How many pounds of pecans did Eduardo sell in total?

  1. 363 ✓
  2. 353
  3. 372
  4. 350

Why: To find the total amount of pecans sold by Eduardo, add the amount he sold in the morning to the amount he sold in the afternoon: 125 + 238 = 363. Therefore, Eduardo sold a total of 363 pounds of pecans.

5

Diego has a box of crayons. He has 12 crayons that are blue, red, and green. If he has 5 red crayons and 4 blue crayons, how many green crayons does he have?

  1. 3 ✓
  2. 5
  3. 4
  4. 2

Why: To find out how many green crayons Diego has, we can start by adding the number of red and blue crayons together. Diego has 5 red crayons and 4 blue crayons, so 5 + 4 equals 9. To find the number of green crayons, we subtract this total from the total number of crayons: 12 - 9 equals 3. Therefore, Diego has 3 green crayons.

  2. 22

  4. 24

  4. $6
