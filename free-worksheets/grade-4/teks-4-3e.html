Imani has 3/4 of a pizza left after her birthday party. If she wants to share the remaining pizza equally among 3 friends, how much pizza will each friend get?

  1. 1/4 ✓
  2. 1/3
  3. 1/2
  4. 3/4

Why: To find out how much pizza each friend will get, we divide the remaining pizza, which is 3/4, by 3. This can be calculated as (3/4) ÷ 3, or (3/4) × (1/3). Multiplying the numerators gives us 3 * 1 = 3, and multiplying the denominators gives us 4 * 3 = 12. So, we have 3/12, which simplifies to 1/4. Therefore, each friend will get 1/4 of the pizza.

Mia went to a local farmers market in the DFW Metroplex and bought some delicious fruit. She purchased 2/5 of a watermelon and 1/5 of a cantaloupe. How much fruit did Mia buy in total?

  1. 3/5 ✓
  2. 1/5
  3. 2/5
  4. 4/5

Why: To find the total amount of fruit Mia bought, you need to add the fractions: 2/5 (watermelon) + 1/5 (cantaloupe) = (2 + 1)/5 = 3/5. Thus, Mia bought a total of 3/5 of the fruit.

  1. 1 cup ✓
  2. 3/2 cup
  3. 1/2 cup
  4. 1/4 cup

Why: To find the total amount of cheese and salsa, we add the two fractions: 3/4 + 1/4. Since the denominators are the same, we can simply add the numerators: 3 + 1 = 4. This gives us 4/4, which is equal to 1 cup. Therefore, the correct answer is 1 cup.

  1. 5/8 ✓
  2. 3/8
  3. 1/4
  4. 1/2

Why: Sofia started with a whole chocolate bar, which is represented as 8/8. She gave away 3 pieces, which leaves her with 5 pieces. To find the fraction of the chocolate bar she has left, we can subtract 3/8 from 8/8. That means 8/8 - 3/8 = 5/8. Therefore, Sofia has 5/8 of the chocolate bar left.
