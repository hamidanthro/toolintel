Imani is collecting data about the number of books read by students in her class during the summer. The following table shows the number of books read by five students: Carlos - 10 books, Sofia - 15 books, Chen - 8 books, Tatiana - 12 books, and Imani - 20 books. What is the average number of books read by the students in Imani's class?

  1. 13 ✓
  2. 12
  3. 15
  4. 10

Why: To find the average number of books read, first add the number of books read by all the students: 10 + 15 + 8 + 12 + 20 = 65. Then, divide the total by the number of students (5): 65 / 5 = 13. Therefore, the average number of books read by the students is 13.

Lila is collecting data on the number of flowers in her garden over five weeks. The data collected is as follows: Week 1: 8 flowers, Week 2: 10 flowers, Week 3: 6 flowers, Week 4: 12 flowers, Week 5: 14 flowers. What is the average number of flowers Lila had in her garden over the five weeks?

  1. 10 ✓
  2. 11
  3. 12
  4. 9

Why: To find the average number of flowers, add the total number of flowers from all five weeks: 8 + 10 + 6 + 12 + 14 = 50. Then, divide this total by the number of weeks: 50 / 5 = 10. Therefore, the average number of flowers in Lila's garden over these weeks is 10.
