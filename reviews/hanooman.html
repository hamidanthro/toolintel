üí° What the marketing page doesn't tell you

1. Multilingual != equal performance. While Hanooman supports 11 Indian languages, performance varies significantly. Hindi and Telugu show stronger results; less-resourced languages like Konkani lag behind. No published benchmarks per language.

2. "India-focused" means trade-offs. The model's training emphasis on Indian data comes at the cost of global knowledge. Western pop culture, international business contexts, and English-dominant technical domains show weaker comprehension.

3. Enterprise readiness is questionable. Limited public case studies, no SOC 2 certification mentioned, and sparse compliance documentation. Fine for pilots, risky for production.

Real-World Use Cases We Tested

üè™ Indian E-commerce

What we used it for: Product descriptions in Hindi and Tamil, customer support responses, regional marketing copy.

What worked: Native language quality was solid for Hindi product descriptions. Cultural references felt authentic. Code-switching (Hindi-English mix) handled naturally.

What didn't: Translation inconsistencies between languages. Tamil output occasionally felt machine-translated despite native training. Limited control over formal vs. colloquial tone.

üì∞ Regional Content Creation

What we used it for: News summaries, blog post generation, social media content in Marathi and Bengali.

What worked: Local news contexts understood well. Regional festivals and cultural events accurately referenced. Handles Devanagari and other scripts cleanly.

What didn't: Fact-checking burden remains high‚Äîhallucinations present like any LLM. Tone inconsistencies across long-form content. Limited nuance in sensitive political/religious topics.

üíº Customer Service (Banking/Telecom)

What we used it for: Hindi/Telugu customer query responses, FAQ generation, complaint drafting.

What worked: Basic query handling in regional languages effective. Sentiment detection adequate. Speed comparable to global models.

What didn't: Complex financial or technical queries often required fallback to English. No dedicated compliance mode for regulated industries. Integration challenges (limited SDK examples).

Pricing Analysis

Note: Pricing details are limited as of Feb 2026. Model access is primarily through partnerships and pilot programs. Public API pricing not yet established.

Expected Positioning: BharatGPT has indicated competitive pricing targeting the Indian market, likely undercutting global players. Early access pricing reportedly 30-50% below OpenAI/Anthropic for similar model sizes.

üí∞ What buyers get wrong about emerging regional LLMs

Lower price != better value. Cheaper per-token costs mean nothing if you need 3x more iterations to get usable output. Factor in engineering time for prompt tuning and quality assurance.

Pilot pricing vanishes at scale. Early-access discounts and partnership deals often expire once you're locked in. Verify production pricing in writing before committing infrastructure resources.
