üí° What the marketing page doesn't tell you

1. Jamba's "efficiency" doesn't translate to cost savings. Despite the SSM architecture being computationally cheaper, AI21's pricing is similar to GPT-4. You're not getting a discount for using a more efficient model.

2. Wordtune is the real product. AI21's consumer success comes from Wordtune (writing assistant), not their API business. The API feels like a side product in comparison.

3. "30% more text per token" is marketing speak. They count tokens differently than OpenAI, so direct comparison is misleading. In practice, costs are comparable.

Real-World Use Cases We Tested

‚úçÔ∏è Content Writing

What we used it for: Blog post generation, paraphrasing, summarization of long documents.

What worked: Creative writing quality was good. Paraphrasing API produced natural variations. Summarization handled 50-page documents well.

What didn't: Output sometimes too verbose. No native plagiarism checking. Wordtune subscription separate from API access (double cost).

üìö Long Document Analysis

What we used it for: Contract review, research paper summarization.

What worked: 256K context handled full contracts without chunking. Jamba's SSM architecture processed long inputs faster than pure transformers.

What didn't: Reasoning accuracy lower than Claude on complex contracts. Missed subtle clauses that required multi-step logic.
