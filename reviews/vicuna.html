ðŸ’¡ What the marketing doesn't tell you

1. The "90% ChatGPT quality" claim is from GPT-4's evaluation. An LLM judging another LLM. Not humans. In our testing, Vicuna's conversational quality is closer to 70-75% of ChatGPT-3.5 (2023 version). It struggles with nuance, multi-step reasoning, and often gives verbose, unfocused answers.

2. Training cost was $300 in 2023. In 2026, it's legacy tech. Vicuna-13B is built on LLaMA v1, which has been surpassed by LLaMA 2, LLaMA 3, and countless fine-tunes. You'd be deploying a 3-year-old model that newer 7B models outperform.

3. Non-commercial license = dealbreaker for business use. Vicuna inherits LLaMA v1's non-commercial restriction. You CANNOT use this for any commercial application. Fine for research and tinkering, but useless for startups or enterprises looking to ship products.

What We Tested

  • Model: Vicuna-13B v1.5
  • Infrastructure: Single A100 (40GB)
  • Benchmarks: MT-Bench, human conversational quality, instruction following
  • Duration: 5 days testing (Feb 2026)
  • Cost: $120 cloud compute

Performance Reality Check

  • Conversational: Decent for casual chat, struggles with complex instructions
  • Instruction following: 65-70% success rate on multi-step tasks
  • Inference speed: 18-22 tokens/sec (A100)
  • Context window: 2048 tokens (limited for modern use cases)
  • Outdated: Surpassed by Llama 2, Mistral 7B, and newer fine-tunes
