⚠️ CRITICAL: Read Before Using

Dolphin models have NO safety filtering. They will generate harmful, illegal, and unethical content without refusal. This is intentional for research purposes.

Do NOT use for: Customer-facing applications, chatbots, content generation for public consumption, or any scenario where output is not carefully reviewed.

Legal risk: You are responsible for all outputs. Using uncensored models in production can expose you to liability for harmful content generation.

  • Lower quality: Uncensoring process degrades base model

What "Uncensored" Actually Means

Dolphin models are created by:

  1. Taking a base model (Llama, Mistral, etc.)
  2. Removing safety training data (filtering out refusal examples)
  3. Fine-tuning on uncensored datasets (teaching it to answer everything)

Result: A model that acts like the base model before safety fine-tuning was applied.

In Practice

  • ✗ Won't refuse harmful requests
  • ✗ Generates biased content more freely
  • ✗ No content warnings or ethical considerations
  • ✓ Useful for testing what safety filters actually prevent
  • ✓ Reveals base model capabilities without alignment

Benchmark Performance

Technical quality is comparable to base models (Llama/Mistral), but:

  • Reasoning: Slightly degraded vs base (~5% lower on MMLU)
  • Coding: Similar to base model performance
  • Safety benchmarks: Fails deliberately (by design)

Trade-off: Removing safety training also removes some helpful behaviors (politeness, nuance, ethical reasoning).

Legitimate Use Cases

1. AI Safety Research

Testing how well your custom safety layer works by throwing it against an intentionally unsafe model.

2. Red-Teaming

Discovering attack vectors that could jailbreak commercial models. Dolphin shows what those models are hiding.

3. Academic Study

Research papers on alignment, bias, and safety filtering effectiveness. Comparing filtered vs unfiltered outputs.

4. Creative Writing (Private)

Some fiction authors use it for unconstrained storytelling. Must remain private.

Legal & Ethical Considerations

If you deploy Dolphin in a product:

  • You are liable for all generated content
  • No "AI did it" defense — you chose to remove safety filters
  • Platform TOS violations likely (AWS, Azure, Google ban unsafe AI)
  • Reputation damage when (not if) it generates harmful content
  • Potential criminal liability depending on use case and jurisdiction

Bottom Line

Dolphin models are powerful research tools for understanding AI safety, but should never be deployed in customer-facing applications without extensive safety wrapping.

Use Dolphin ONLY if: You're doing legitimate AI safety research, red-teaming, or academic work on alignment.

Absolutely do NOT use if: You're building a product, chatbot, or any tool that generates content for others. Use Claude, GPT-4, or safety-filtered open models instead.

Final warning: The existence of uncensored models is controversial. Many in the AI safety community believe they should not be publicly available. If you use them irresponsibly, you contribute to the problem.
