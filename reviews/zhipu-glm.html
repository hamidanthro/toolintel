üí° What the marketing page doesn't tell you

1. English performance is mediocre. While ChatGLM markets itself as bilingual, English accuracy and fluency lag 2-3 generations behind GPT-4. Fine for basic tasks, not suitable for professional English content.

2. Data stays in China. All API requests are processed on mainland servers. For businesses subject to GDPR or other data sovereignty laws, this is a dealbreaker.

3. Content filtering is aggressive. Expect frequent refusals on politically sensitive topics, even in technical contexts. Censorship extends beyond Chinese politics to global current events.

Real-World Use Cases We Tested

üá®üá≥ Chinese Market Business

What we used it for: Customer service chatbot in Mandarin, product descriptions, social media content for Weibo/WeChat.

What worked: Excellent tone and cultural awareness. Natural idiom usage. Great at formal vs. casual register switching. Pricing was 1/3 of GPT-4 API costs.

What didn't: Required Chinese business registration for API access. Documentation assumes familiarity with Aliyun/Tencent Cloud. Limited examples for English developers.

üìö Education & Research

What we used it for: Teaching assistant for computer science courses, research paper summarization, code explanation in Chinese.

What worked: Open-source ChatGLM-6B runs locally on university hardware. Good balance of capability and resource requirements. Free tier generous for academic use.

What didn't: English technical documentation spotty. Some CS concepts better explained in English by GPT-4.
