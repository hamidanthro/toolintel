üí° What the marketing page doesn't tell you

1. "Bilingual" is real, not marketing. Unlike most Chinese models that bolt on English as an afterthought, Yi was trained equally on both languages from the start. English performance genuinely rivals GPT-3.5.

2. Open-source models are the full product. The API versions are the exact same weights you can download. No "crippled open-source" games.

3. Context window is slow. That 200K context works, but inference time at max context is 3-5x slower than smaller contexts. Budget accordingly.

Real-World Use Cases We Tested

üåê Translation & Localization

What we used it for: E-commerce product descriptions (CN‚ÜîEN), technical documentation translation, customer support in both languages.

What worked: Best Chinese-English translation quality we've tested from a Chinese model. Catches idioms and cultural context. Maintains technical accuracy across languages.

What didn't: Still not perfect on highly technical jargon. Occasional awkward phrasing in English that a native speaker would catch.

üìÑ Document Analysis

What we used it for: Long-form contract review, research paper summarization, codebase analysis.

What worked: 200K context handled full documents without chunking. Maintained coherence across entire context. Competitive with Claude on long-form reasoning.

What didn't: Slower inference at high context. Cost per token higher than shorter-context models.

üíª Local Deployment

What we used it for: Self-hosted chatbot for privacy-sensitive research lab.

What worked: Apache 2.0 license means true ownership. Yi-34B runs on 2x A100 GPUs (accessible for universities). Quantized versions (4-bit) run on consumer hardware.

What didn't: Still requires significant compute. Consumer-grade GPUs (RTX 4090) struggle with full 34B model.
