üí° What the marketing page doesn't tell you

1. It's a consumer app company that happens to have an AI model. MiniMax's real business is Glow (AI companion) and Hailuo (video gen). The ABAB API feels like an afterthought.

2. Voice features are the killer app. The text model is mid-tier, but voice synthesis rivals ElevenLabs at 1/5 the cost. That's the real value prop.

3. "Global" availability is marketing. Core features require Chinese phone number, payment methods, and sometimes VPN. International access is limited.

Real-World Use Cases We Tested

üéôÔ∏è Voice Applications

What we used it for: Podcast narration, character voices for games, customer service IVR.

What worked: Exceptional voice quality. Emotional range impressed testers. Voice cloning from 30-second sample worked well. Significantly cheaper than Western alternatives.

What didn't: English voices noticeably worse than Chinese. Occasional audio artifacts. No fine-grained control (pitch, speed limited).

üì± Consumer App

What we used it for: AI companion chatbot, interactive story generation.

What worked: Character consistency across conversations. Creative story beats. Natural personality. Good at roleplaying scenarios.

What didn't: Factual accuracy poor. Math and logic worse than GPT-3.5. Can't handle technical queries.
