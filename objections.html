Every Review Is Written by a Human. Humans Make Mistakes. Here Is How We Fix Them.

If you believe a review of your product contains a factual error, you have the right to submit a formal objection. All objections are reviewed seriously and responded to in writing within 14 business days. Scores and assessments that are factually incorrect will be corrected and the correction will be logged publicly. Scores and assessments you simply disagree with will not be changed ‚Äî but your objection will be published in full alongside the review.

What This Process Is ‚Äî and What It Is Not

What this process is: A mechanism for correcting genuine factual errors made by a human reviewer.

What this process is not: An appeal process. A negotiation. A way to soften scores. A route to removing unfavorable assessments.

If your objection is a factual correction with evidence, submit it and we will take it seriously. If your objection is that you believe your tool deserved a higher score, the appropriate response is the Vendor Response window available to all reviewed companies ‚Äî not the objection process.

Accepted Objection Types

Objections must fall into one of the following categories:

Factual Error

Verifiably incorrect information such as wrong pricing, wrong certification status, or wrong feature description.

Outdated Information

Information that was accurate at review time but has since changed.

Testing Methodology Concern

A specific concern about how a particular test was conducted, with evidence.

Material Omission

A significant verifiable fact that was not included and would materially affect the score.

Note: "Score Disagreement" is intentionally not on this list. If you disagree with an assessment but cannot identify a specific factual error, your objection will not be processed through this system.

Objection Review Process

1

Submission Logged

Your objection is received and assigned a reference number within 24 hours. You'll receive confirmation via email.

2

Initial Review

The editorial team assesses whether the objection falls within the accepted objection types. Objections that are score appeals disguised as factual corrections are identified and classified at this stage.

3

Evidence Evaluation

The specific claim and supporting evidence are independently verified against primary sources ‚Äî the same standard applied in the original review.

4

Decision

One of three outcomes is reached: Correction Issued if the objection identified a genuine factual error, Partially Upheld if some elements were factually incorrect but the overall assessment stands, or Not Upheld if the evidence does not support the claim of factual error.

5

Publication

The decision and full reasoning are published regardless of outcome. All objections become part of the permanent record.

Objection Outcomes

‚úì Correction Issued

  • The review is updated with the corrected information
  • The correction is clearly marked on the review page with a note: "Correction issued [date] following vendor objection ‚Äî see correction log for details"
  • The score is recalculated if the factual correction materially affects any scoring category
  • The original text is preserved in the correction log so readers can see exactly what changed and why
  • The full objection and ToolIntel response are published in a dedicated Objection Record section at the bottom of the review

‚óê Partially Upheld

  • Specific factual corrections are made where warranted
  • Both the corrections and the maintained assessments are documented
  • The full record is published showing what was corrected and what was maintained

‚úó Not Upheld

  • The review remains unchanged
  • The vendor's full objection is published in the Objection Record section of the review alongside the full ToolIntel response explaining why the objection was not upheld
  • A note is added to the review: "A formal objection was submitted by the vendor on [date] and reviewed. The editorial assessment was maintained. See Objection Record for full details."
