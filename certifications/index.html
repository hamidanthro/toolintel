Certification Directory

All certifications tracked by ToolIntel, with plain-English explanations of what each means and how we verify it.

üîí Security & Privacy

SOC 2 Type I

Issued by: Licensed CPA firms

A point-in-time audit that evaluates whether a company's security controls are properly designed. It's a snapshot assessment ‚Äî it says "on this date, these controls existed" but doesn't confirm they work consistently over time. Typically takes 2-4 months to obtain. Does not guarantee ongoing security; Type II is more rigorous.

Verification: We contact the auditing firm directly to confirm the report exists and covers the stated period.

SOC 2 Type II

Issued by: Licensed CPA firms

A continuous audit over 6-12 months that evaluates whether security controls work effectively over time ‚Äî not just that they exist. Significantly more rigorous than Type I. Typically takes 6-12 months after Type I. The gold standard for SaaS security assurance in enterprise procurement.

Verification: We contact the auditing firm directly and confirm both the report existence and the audit period coverage.

ISO 27001

Issued by: Accredited certification bodies

An international standard for information security management systems (ISMS). Requires establishing formal policies, risk assessment processes, and controls across the organization. Valid for 3 years with annual surveillance audits. Widely recognized globally, especially in European procurement.

Verification: We search the accreditation body's public certificate database to confirm active certification status.

ISO 27701

Issued by: Accredited certification bodies

An extension to ISO 27001 specifically for privacy information management. Helps organizations demonstrate GDPR and other privacy regulation compliance. Requires existing ISO 27001 certification as a prerequisite. Increasingly important for tools handling personal data.

Verification: Verified through accreditation body certificate search, confirming both ISO 27701 and prerequisite ISO 27001.

CSA STAR

Issued by: Cloud Security Alliance

Cloud-specific security certification with three levels: Self-Assessment (Level 1), Third-Party Audit (Level 2), and Continuous Monitoring (Level 3). The STAR Registry is publicly searchable. Particularly relevant for cloud-native AI tools and SaaS platforms.

Verification: We search the public CSA STAR Registry at cloudsecurityalliance.org/star/registry.

üè• Healthcare

HIPAA BAA

Contractual agreement (not issued by a body)

A Business Associate Agreement is a legal contract required when a vendor handles Protected Health Information (PHI) on behalf of a healthcare organization. Not a certification per se ‚Äî it's a willingness to sign a legally binding agreement. Essential for any AI tool used in US healthcare contexts.

Verification: We request a sample BAA from the vendor and confirm it meets HHS requirements for required provisions.

HITRUST CSF

Issued by: HITRUST Alliance

A comprehensive, certifiable framework that harmonizes healthcare and security regulations including HIPAA, NIST, and ISO 27001. Three certification levels: e1 (essential), i1 (implemented), and r2 (risk-based). R2 is the most rigorous. Takes 6-18 months depending on level.

Verification: We search the HITRUST Central portal or contact HITRUST directly to confirm certification status and level.

FDA 21 CFR Part 11

Compliance standard (FDA)

Requirements for electronic records and signatures in FDA-regulated industries. Not a certification but a compliance standard ‚Äî vendors self-attest to compliance. Critical for AI tools used in pharmaceutical, medical device, or clinical trial contexts.

Verification: We review vendor's Part 11 compliance documentation and technical controls against FDA requirements.

HITECH Compliance

Compliance standard (HHS)

The HITECH Act strengthens HIPAA enforcement and adds breach notification requirements. Compliance means the vendor follows enhanced data protection practices and will notify appropriately in case of breach. Often demonstrated alongside HIPAA BAA availability.

Verification: Reviewed as part of HIPAA BAA verification, confirming breach notification procedures are documented.

FedRAMP Authorized

Issued by: FedRAMP PMO / JAB

The federal government's standardized approach to cloud security assessment. Authorization means the product has been assessed against NIST 800-53 controls and approved for federal agency use. Three impact levels: Low, Moderate, High. Takes 12-18 months on average.

Verification: We confirm status directly through the official FedRAMP Marketplace at marketplace.fedramp.gov.

FedRAMP In Process

Listed by: FedRAMP PMO

"In Process" means a vendor is actively working toward FedRAMP authorization with an agency sponsor. Not yet authorized, but indicates commitment and progress. Useful for procurement planning but cannot be used for federal contracts requiring full authorization.

Verification: Confirmed through FedRAMP Marketplace "In Process" listing with sponsoring agency identified.

StateRAMP

Issued by: StateRAMP PMO

A standardized security authorization program for state and local government cloud adoption. Similar framework to FedRAMP but tailored for non-federal government. Growing in importance as states formalize cloud procurement requirements.

Verification: We search the StateRAMP Authorized Product List at stateramp.org.

CMMC (Level 1-3)

Issued by: CMMC Accreditation Body

Cybersecurity Maturity Model Certification required for Department of Defense contractors. Level 1 is basic cyber hygiene, Level 2 aligns with NIST 800-171, Level 3 requires advanced practices. Essential for any AI tool handling Controlled Unclassified Information (CUI).

Verification: We confirm through the CMMC-AB Marketplace or by contacting the certified third-party assessment organization (C3PAO).

GDPR Compliance Documentation

Self-attested (EU regulation)

GDPR is a regulation, not a certification ‚Äî there is no official "GDPR Certified" status. What we verify is that the vendor has documented Data Processing Agreements, privacy policies, data subject rights procedures, and appointed a DPO if required. Necessary for any tool processing EU resident data.

Verification: We review DPA availability, privacy policy completeness, and documented data subject rights procedures.

CCPA Compliance Documentation

Self-attested (California regulation)

California Consumer Privacy Act compliance requires specific disclosures, opt-out mechanisms, and data handling procedures. Like GDPR, no official certification exists. We verify documented procedures and consumer rights mechanisms are in place.

Verification: We review privacy policy for CCPA-specific disclosures and confirm opt-out mechanisms are functional.

PIPEDA Compliance

Self-attested (Canadian regulation)

Canada's federal private sector privacy law. Compliance requires fair information principles including consent, limiting collection, and accountability. Important for AI tools with Canadian users or handling Canadian personal information.

Verification: We review privacy policy and documented procedures against PIPEDA's 10 fair information principles.

LGPD Compliance

Self-attested (Brazilian regulation)

Brazil's General Data Protection Law, similar in structure to GDPR. Requires legal basis for processing, data subject rights, and security measures. Increasingly important for AI tools with Brazilian market presence.

Verification: We review documented legal bases, DPO appointment (if required), and data subject rights procedures.

PDPA Compliance

Self-attested (Singapore/Thailand regulations)

Personal Data Protection Acts in Singapore and Thailand establish consent-based frameworks for personal data processing. Requirements vary by jurisdiction but generally include notification, consent, and security obligations.

Verification: We review documented compliance procedures against the specific PDPA requirements of claimed jurisdictions.

ü§ñ AI Specific

EU AI Act Conformity Assessment

Issued by: EU Notified Bodies

For high-risk AI systems under the EU AI Act, conformity assessments will be required starting 2025-2026. Assessments evaluate risk management, data governance, transparency, human oversight, and accuracy. The most significant AI-specific compliance requirement globally.

Verification: We confirm through the notified body that conducted the assessment and check the EU database once operational.

NIST AI RMF Alignment

Self-attested (NIST framework)

The NIST AI Risk Management Framework provides voluntary guidance for managing AI risks. Not a certification, but vendors can document alignment with its Govern, Map, Measure, and Manage functions. Increasingly referenced in US federal AI procurement.

Verification: We review vendor's documented alignment to specific AI RMF functions and subcategories.

IEEE CertifAIEd

Issued by: IEEE Standards Association

A mark program assessing AI systems against criteria including transparency, accountability, algorithmic bias, and privacy. Multiple specialty marks available. One of the first third-party AI ethics certification programs with global recognition.

Verification: We confirm through IEEE's CertifAIEd program registry and verify mark validity.

ISO 42001

Issued by: Accredited certification bodies

The first international standard specifically for AI management systems. Establishes requirements for responsible AI development and deployment including risk assessment, transparency, and human oversight. Published in 2023, adoption is growing rapidly.

Verification: We search accreditation body certificate databases to confirm active ISO 42001 certification.

How We Verify Certifications

Our verification process for each certification type:

SOC 2 Reports
Contact the auditing firm directly and confirm the report exists, covers the stated period, and was issued to the named entity.
ISO Certificates
Search the accreditation body's public certificate database to confirm the certificate is active and covers the stated scope.
HIPAA BAA
Request a sample Business Associate Agreement from the vendor and confirm it includes all HHS-required provisions.
FedRAMP
Confirm status through the official FedRAMP Marketplace at marketplace.fedramp.gov, verifying authorization level and sponsoring agency.
EU AI Act
Contact the notified body that conducted the conformity assessment and verify through the EU database once operational.
No Public Registry
For certifications without a public registry, we contact the issuing body directly, document their response, and archive the verification evidence.
Important: If we cannot independently verify a certification through a primary source, it will not display as "Verified" on this platform ‚Äî regardless of how credible the documentation appears.
