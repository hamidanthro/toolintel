Correction Policy

We make mistakes. Here's how we fix themâ€”publicly, promptly, and permanently on the record.

Our Commitment

Every review we publish represents our best judgment at the time. But we're human, and errors happen. When they do, we fix them openly. No quiet edits. No memory-holing mistakes. Every change is logged and explained.

Types of Corrections

Factual Errors

Incorrect statements about a product's features, pricing, policies, or capabilities. These are corrected immediately upon verification.

"We stated the free tier includes 100 API calls; it actually includes 1,000."

Score Adjustments

Changes to numerical scores based on new information, product updates, or methodology revisions. Original scores are preserved with strikethrough.

"Data Privacy score updated from 72 to 81 after vendor provided SOC2 certification."

Clarifications

Additional context that doesn't change conclusions but improves accuracy or nuance. These are added inline with [Clarification] tags.

"[Clarification: This limitation applies only to the Starter tier.]"

Retractions

In rare cases where a review contains fundamental errors that undermine its validity, we retract and replace it. The original remains archived.

"This review has been retracted. A revised review was published on [date]."
